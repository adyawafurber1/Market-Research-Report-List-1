<p><ul><li>Dead Burnt Dolomite</li><li>Light Burnt Dolomite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The burnt dolomite market is divided into two primary types: Dead Burnt Dolomite (DBD) and Light Burnt Dolomite (LBD). Dead Burnt Dolomite is produced by calcining dolomite at high temperatures, resulting in a product with higher stability and lower reactivity, commonly used in steelmaking and refractory applications. In contrast, Light Burnt Dolomite is obtained through lower temperature processing, leading to a more reactive product, primarily employed in agriculture and chemical industries for soil conditioning and as a pH regulator.</p></p>

<p><p>Gesture recognition for mobile devices can be categorized into two main types: 2D and 3D gesture recognition. 2D gesture recognition primarily involves detecting movements on a flat surface, such as swipes or taps on a touchscreen. It enhances user interaction by providing intuitive controls. In contrast, 3D gesture recognition captures motion in a three-dimensional space, enabling more complex gestures, like hand movements in front of a camera. This technology allows for advanced applications in gaming, augmented reality, and hands-free control.</p></p>

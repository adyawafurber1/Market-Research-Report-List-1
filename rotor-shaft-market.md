<p><p>The rotor shaft market comprises two primary types: the standard process and the hot isostatic pressing (HIP) process. The standard process involves traditional manufacturing techniques, such as forging and machining, which form rotor shafts through established methods. In contrast, the HIP process enhances material properties by applying high pressure and temperature to eliminate porosity, resulting in improved performance and durability. Both processes cater to various industrial applications, emphasizing efficiency and reliability in rotor shaft production.</p></p>

<p><p>GIS substations can be categorized into High Voltage (HV) and Ultra High Voltage (UHV) markets based on their operational voltage levels. High Voltage substations typically operate between 100 kV and 800 kV, accommodating regional power distribution efficiently. In contrast, Ultra High Voltage substations exceed 800 kV, designed for long-distance transmission of large power loads with minimal losses. Both types utilize advanced gas-insulated technology, offering compact designs suitable for urban settings while improving reliability and safety in electrical infrastructures.</p></p>

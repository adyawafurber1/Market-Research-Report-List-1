<p><strong>The DNA-Microarray for Agriculture Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Oligonucleotide DNA Microarrays (oDNA)</li><li>Complementary DNA Microarrays (cDNA)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>DNA microarrays are powerful tools in agriculture for analyzing gene expression and identifying genetic variations. Oligonucleotide DNA microarrays (oDNA) use short, synthetic DNA sequences to probe specific genes, allowing researchers to monitor gene activity and detect mutations. Complementary DNA microarrays (cDNA) involve reverse transcription, where RNA is converted into cDNA for hybridization on a microarray. Both types enable comprehensive analysis of plant responses to environmental stresses, disease resistance, and overall crop improvement, supporting precision agriculture and enhancing yield.</p></p>

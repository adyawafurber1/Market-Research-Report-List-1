<p><p>The mutual inductor market can be categorized into three main types: electromagnetic, capacitive, and optoelectronic. Electromagnetic mutual inductors utilize magnetic fields to transfer energy between coils, commonly found in transformers and inductive charging systems. Capacitive mutual inductors rely on electric fields for energy transfer, often used in RF applications. Optoelectronic mutual inductors combine optical and electronic components, enabling data transmission through light, ideal for high-speed communication systems. Each type serves distinct applications, targeting specific technological needs.</p></p>
